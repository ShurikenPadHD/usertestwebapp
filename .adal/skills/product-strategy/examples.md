# Product Strategy Examples

Here are realistic examples of how to use this skill:

---

## Example 1: B2B SaaS — AI Meeting Notes for Sales Teams

**User Query:**
> "I'm building an AI product that automatically generates meeting notes and action items for sales teams. It's aimed at B2B SaaS companies with 20-100 employees. Help me define my product strategy."

**Expected Output:**
- **ICP Profile:** \"Alex the Account Executive\" — 28-40 years old, manages 8-15 deals quarterly, uses Salesforce + Zoom. Pain point: 30% of time spent on admin work. Budget: $50-100/month.
- **Top 3 Problems:** (1) Lost action items after calls, (2) Forgetting to follow up with prospects, (3) Wasting time on manual note-taking.
- **Positioning Statement:** For sales reps who need to maximize selling time, SalesScribe is an AI assistant that automatically captures and structures meeting insights, so reps can focus on closing deals.
- **Differentiation:** vs. Gong (analytics focus), vs. Otter.ai (generic transcription), vs. manual notes (speed + action items).

## Example 2: Developer Tool — API Monitoring for Startups

**User Query:**
> "I want to build an API monitoring tool for startups that don't have dedicated DevOps teams. What should my product strategy be?"

**Expected Output:**
- **ICP Profile:** \"Jordan the Full-Stack Dev\" — 25-35, works at a startup with 5-20 engineers, owns both backend and frontend. Uses Vercel, AWS, or Railway.
- **Positioning Statement:** For full-stack developers who need reliable APIs without the ops overhead, APIPulse is a monitoring platform that alerts you before customers notice downtime.
- **Value Prop Stack:** (1) Instant setup (no agent), (2) Slack/Discord alerts, (3) Error trace visualization, (4) Cost-effective for startups.
- **Differentiation:** vs. Datadog (enterprise-focused), vs. Sentry (error tracking focus), vs. Prometheus (self-hosted, complex).

## Example 3: SMB SaaS — Automated Bookkeeping for Restaurants

**User Query:**
> "I'm building accounting software for small restaurant owners (1-5 locations). Help me figure out my positioning."

**Expected Output:**
- **ICP Profile:** \"Maria the Restaurant Owner\" — 35-55, owns 1-2 restaurants, uses QuickBooks but finds it confusing. Hates doing books on weekends.
- **Problem:** Pain 9/10 — cash flow blindspots, messy receipts, tax season stress.
- **Category:** \"Automated bookkeeping for restaurants\" — not generic accounting.
- **Positioning Statement:** For restaurant owners who want accurate books without the headache, RestoBooks automates transaction categorization so you can focus on running your kitchen.
- **Messaging Pillars:** (1) \"Made for restaurants, not spreadsheets\" (2) \"Weekends off, finally\" (3) \"Know your numbers in real-time\"
